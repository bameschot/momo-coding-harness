# Story Design

## Overview
This document outlines the design for a 10-paragraph story. The goal is to create an engaging narrative that captures the reader's attention and conveys a meaningful message.

## Goals / Non-Goals
**Goals:**
- Create a compelling beginning, middle, and end.
- Develop well-rounded characters with clear motivations.
- Build tension and resolve it satisfactorily.
- Ensure coherence and flow throughout the story.

**Non-Goals:**
- Writing a complex or overly detailed plot.
- Including an excessive number of characters or subplots.
- Focusing on a specific genre (e.g., fantasy, sci-fi) unless requested.

## Design
### Paragraph 1: Introduction
- Introduce the protagonist and their ordinary world.
- Establish the setting and tone of the story.
- Hint at the central conflict or challenge.

### Paragraph 2: Inciting Incident
- Present an event that disrupts the protagonist's life.
- Clearly define what is at stake.
- Show the protagonist's initial reaction to the change.

### Paragraph 3: Decision to Act
- The protagonist decides to take action or face a challenge.
- Introduce secondary characters who may aid or oppose the protagonist.
- Begin building tension toward the main conflict.

### Paragraph 4: Rising Action
- Develop the plot with obstacles and setbacks.
- Show the protagonist's growth or adaptation to challenges.
- Introduce subplots or additional layers of conflict if needed.

### Paragraph 5: Midpoint Twist
- Present a significant turning point in the story.
- Reveal new information that changes the protagonist's understanding of the situation.
- Raise the stakes further and increase tension.

### Paragraph 6: Deepening Conflict
- Escalate the main conflict with more intense challenges.
- Show the protagonist struggling but persevering.
- Introduce a moment of doubt or vulnerability.

### Paragraph 7: All Is Lost Moment
- The protagonist faces their greatest setback yet.
- It seems as though all hope is lost, and the goal cannot be achieved.
- This moment should feel emotionally impactful for the reader.

### Paragraph 8: Climax
- The protagonist makes a final, decisive effort to overcome the conflict.
- Show their growth and determination in this pivotal moment.
- Resolve the central tension of the story.

### Paragraph 9: Falling Action
- Tie up loose ends from subplots or secondary conflicts.
- Show the immediate aftermath of the climax.
- Begin transitioning toward the resolution.

### Paragraph 10: Resolution
- Conclude the story with a sense of closure.
- Reflect on the protagonist's journey and growth.
- Leave the reader with a final thought or emotion, such as hope, satisfaction, or reflection.
